<caesura>

Break, pause, or interruption in the normal tempo of a composition. Typically indicated by "railroad tracks", i.e., two diagonal slashes.

Remarks

The caesura often indicates an abrupt interruption in the performance followed by an equally sudden resumption. Its duration is typically shorter than a grand pause (G.P.) or long pause (L.P.), but longer than that indicated by a breath mark. When combined with a fermata a longer silence is usually implied. The starting point of the caesura may be indicated by either a startid, tstamp, tstamp.ges, or tstamp.real attribute. It is a semantic error not to specify a starting point attribute.Unlike the breath mark, a caesura may have a performed duration. Also, while the default value for place on the breath mark is "above", the default value for place for a caesura is "within". Unless indicated by other attributes, a caesura will be rendered as a pair of slanted lines through the top line of the staff.
